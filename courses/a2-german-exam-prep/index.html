The Goethe / telc A2 Exam — Format & What to Expect

A2 builds on A1 — longer texts, more complex listening, short formal writing, and more confident conversation.

Lesen (Reading)

3–4 tasks. Everyday texts — notices, short emails, advertisements, simple articles. Tests whether you can extract key information from written everyday German. Time: approximately 25 minutes.

Hören (Listening)

3–4 tasks. Short conversations, announcements, phone messages, played twice. Tests comprehension of spoken everyday German at A2 pace. Time: approximately 20 minutes.

Schreiben (Writing)

1–2 tasks. Write a short structured message — reply to an email, write a note, fill in a form. Requires correct use of A2 grammar and appropriate greeting / sign-off format. Time: approximately 20 minutes.

Sprechen (Speaking)

3 tasks — introduce yourself and give more detail, describe a picture or situation, and have a short dialogue. Graded on fluency, vocabulary range, and accuracy at A2 level.

Both the Goethe-Zertifikat A2 and telc Deutsch A2 test the same CEFR A2 standard and are accepted for Ausbildung applications that require A2. Exam centres across India: Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune.

Common A2 Mistakes Indian Students Make

Patterns Bobby sees repeatedly after 10+ years of coaching — every one is addressed directly in the A2 course.

Confusing Perfekt and Präteritum

A2 requires Perfekt for most spoken and written contexts (with key exceptions). Students who haven't drilled the Perfekt forms of irregular verbs lose marks on both writing and speaking tasks.

Ignoring the Schreiben format

The writing task has a specific format — appropriate greeting, structured body, correct sign-off. Students who write grammatically good German but in the wrong format still lose marks. We practise this from week one.

Freezing in the Sprechen section

Speaking with an examiner or partner feels different from classroom practice. We simulate exam conditions from Month 2 onwards so students are comfortable and confident, not surprised on exam day.

Under-practising Hören

Listening comprehension is harder when you are used to Indian accents. We use authentic Goethe and telc audio from the start — not simplified recordings — so the exam audio sounds familiar, not foreign.

You need to pass each section separately at 60% and achieve an overall pass. The Goethe A2 and telc A2 both follow this section-based pass criterion — you cannot compensate a weak section with a strong one. Our mock exams are designed to identify and fix weak sections before exam day.
A2 requires more vocabulary (approximately 700–1,000 words vs 500 at A1), more complex grammar (past tense, modal verbs, two-way prepositions, separable verbs), longer reading texts, and more structured writing. The speaking tasks also require more than basic self-introduction — you need to describe, respond, and interact.
A2 alone is generally not sufficient for employment visas — most work and Ausbildung visas require B1 minimum. However, some specific sectors (agriculture, hospitality, certain care roles) accept A2. Bobby will advise on your specific employer or visa requirement during the free assessment call.
