About Bobby

I have spent nearly a decade living, studying, and working in Germany. I hold an MBA from the Technische Universität, Berlin. Fluent both in written and spoken German, I have been teaching the language for over 10 years now.

Teaching is complemented by sharing my experience through a virtual tour and tips of life in Germany — making German learning more interesting and relevant. Most language coaching in India treats German like a subject to mug up. What makes BoloGerman different is that real-life context from years in Berlin is woven into every class.

My primary focus is exam preparation for A1, A2, B1, and B2 — structured batches, mock tests, and strategies to help you pass with confidence. All classes are conducted online/offline in small groups. I also support school students (classes 6–10) with curriculum-focused German tuition.

What Makes This Different

I don't run a language institute. I run small online batches — typically 4 to 6 students — where every session is exam-focused and every student gets individual attention. I've sat through German exams, lived in Germany, worked with German colleagues, and watched hundreds of Indian students go through the Goethe and telc process. That experience informs everything about how I teach.

The students who do best at BoloGerman aren't necessarily the most naturally talented. They're the ones who commit to consistent practice between sessions. My job is to make sure every hour they put in is directed at exactly what the examiner is looking for — no wasted effort on things that don't appear in the exam.

"German isn't a qualification to collect. It's a tool that opens real doors — Ausbildung, citizenship, jobs in Germany, family reunification. My students come with a deadline and a goal. I help them get there."

Who I Teach

My students are almost entirely India-based, pursuing German for a specific, practical reason. The most common ones:

  • A1: Au pair visa, family reunification (joining a spouse in Germany)
  • A2/B1: Ausbildung (Germany's vocational training programme)
  • B1: German citizenship, job-seeker visa
  • B2: University admission, professional work visas

Every student comes with a specific exam, a specific deadline, and usually a specific life change waiting on the other side. That context shapes how I approach each batch — the level of urgency, the exam strategy, and the mock test schedule.

The Teaching Setup

All classes are online, via video call. Batches run 2–3 sessions per week, and I keep them small so I can track each student's weak spots. Every student gets access to WhatsApp support between sessions for quick questions. Mock tests under real exam conditions are built into every level — not as an extra, but as the core of exam preparation.

I teach all four levels (A1, A2, B1, B2) for both Goethe and telc. If you're unsure which exam or level is right for your visa or Ausbildung requirement, a free 15-minute call is the fastest way to figure it out.
