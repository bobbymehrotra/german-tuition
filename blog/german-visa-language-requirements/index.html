Germany Visa Language Requirements 2026: Which German Level Do You Need?

Bobby Mehrotra 4 June 2026 7 min read

Germany offers a range of visa categories for Indians looking to study, work, or join family there — and each has its own German language requirement. This is one of the most-asked questions Bobby receives from prospective students: which level do I actually need for my specific visa? Here is the complete 2026 guide, by visa type.

Disclaimer: Visa requirements can change. This guide is based on current German immigration law and embassy practices as of 2026. Always verify the specific requirement for your visa category with the German embassy or consulate in India before registering for an exam.

Ausbildung Visa (Vocational Training)

Required level: B1 (most programmes)

The Ausbildung visa allows Indians to undertake vocational training in Germany — one of the most popular routes for young professionals. The language requirement is set by the employer and the programme, not by the embassy. Most Ausbildung employers and placement consultancies require B1 as the minimum. Some sectors (agriculture, certain hospitality roles) accept A2.

The Goethe-Zertifikat B1 and telc Deutsch B1 are both accepted. The German embassy will ask for proof of language proficiency as part of the visa application — your Goethe or telc certificate is the standard proof.

Student Visa (Bachelor's / Master's in Germany)

Required level: B2 or higher (for German-taught programmes)

If you are applying to a Bachelor's or Master's programme taught in German, you need B2 minimum — most universities set B2 as the language entry requirement. Some competitive programmes expect C1.

The accepted proof of language proficiency for German university admission includes: Goethe-Zertifikat B2/C1, telc Deutsch B2/C1, TestDaF (Test Deutsch als Fremdsprache — an academic German exam specifically designed for university admission), or DSH (Deutsche Sprachprüfung für den Hochschulzugang — taken at German universities).

For English-taught programmes in Germany, German language proficiency is not required for admission — though basic German (A1–A2) is helpful for daily life.

Job Seeker Visa (Chancenkarte)

Required level: B1 (minimum) — B2 recommended

The German job seeker visa (Chancenkarte — Opportunity Card) allows qualified professionals to enter Germany to search for work. The language requirement is B1 minimum for most applicants. However, B2 significantly improves your chances of finding employment once in Germany, and some employers will not interview candidates below B2.

The Chancenkarte is points-based — language level is one of the scoring criteria, and a higher level (B2 vs B1) earns more points, which matters if you are close to the threshold.

Skilled Worker Visa (Fachkräfte-Einwanderungsgesetz)

Required level: B1–B2 depending on profession

Germany's skilled worker immigration law allows qualified professionals in shortage occupations to apply for work visas. The language requirement depends on the profession:

  • IT, engineering, business: B1 is typically sufficient. Many tech employers in Germany operate in English for technical work, but German is needed for daily life and integration.
  • Healthcare (nurses, doctors, care workers): B2 is the standard, and some states require a specific healthcare German exam (telc B2 Pflege or telc B2 Medizin). This is strictly enforced because patient communication is a safety issue.
  • Teaching, law, public sector: C1 in most cases — these roles require near-native German proficiency.

Family Reunion / Spouse Visa

Required level: A1

If you are joining a German citizen or a non-EU national with a German residence permit as a spouse or family member, you need to demonstrate A1 German proficiency before the visa is issued. This is a German immigration law requirement designed to facilitate integration.

The standard accepted exam is the Goethe-Zertifikat A1 (Start Deutsch 1). Some embassies also accept telc A1, but Goethe A1 is the safest choice for this specific visa. The A1 exam is straightforward — most students are ready in 2–3 months with coaching.

Au Pair Visa

Required level: A1 (basic knowledge required)

The Au Pair visa requires basic German knowledge — A1 level is the standard expectation. The German host family and the German Youth Welfare Office (Jugendamt) typically expect you to communicate at a basic level. An A1 certificate strengthens your application significantly.

Which Exam Should You Take for Visa Purposes?

For most visa categories, both Goethe and telc certificates are accepted. The exception is the family reunion / spouse visa, where Goethe A1 (Start Deutsch 1) is the most commonly and explicitly requested exam at German embassies.

For all other visa types, confirm with the specific German consulate or embassy handling your application — requirements can vary slightly between consular regions. The German embassy in New Delhi handles most Indian applications, but consulates in Mumbai, Kolkata, Bangalore, and Chennai have jurisdiction over their respective regions.
